Githeri Recipe

Githeri is a traditional Kenyan dish that is both hearty and nutritious. It is a flavorful combination of boiled beans and corn (maize), cooked together with a blend of spices and vegetables. Githeri is popular across different ethnic groups in Kenya and is often enjoyed as a main course.

How to prepare githeri

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried beans (such as kidney beans or black-eyed peas)
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for boiling

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried beans in water overnight or for at least 6-8 hours. Drain and rinse the beans before cooking.
  2. In a large pot, add the soaked beans and enough water to cover them. Bring to a boil and cook for about 30 minutes to 1 hour until the beans start to soften.
  3. Add the corn kernels to the pot with the beans and continue cooking for another 20-30 minutes until both the beans and corn are tender. Add more water if needed to keep the mixture moist.
  4. In a separate pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  5. Add the minced garlic to the pan and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  6. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they soften and release their juices.
  7. Transfer the tomato and onion mixture to the pot with the beans and corn.
  8. Add the cumin powder, paprika, turmeric powder, and salt to the pot. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  9. Continue cooking the githeri mixture for another 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  10. Adjust the seasoning if needed and serve the githeri hot.

This githeri recipe can serve approximately 4 to 6 people, depending on the portion size and individual appetites. Adjustments can be made to the quantities of ingredients based on the number of servings desired.
