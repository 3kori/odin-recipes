Pilau Recipe

Kenyan pilau is a fragrant and flavorful rice dish that is a staple in Kenyan cuisine. It is known for its aromatic spices and rich taste. The dish typically combines basmati rice with a variety of spices such as cumin, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaf.

How to prepare Pilau

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) bone-in chicken or beef, cut into pieces (optional)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cups water or chicken/beef broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves for garnish (optional)
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and bay leaf. Saut√© for a minute until the spices become fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook until it turns golden brown, stirring occasionally. This should take around 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for an additional minute.
  5. If using meat, add the chicken or beef pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides. If not using meat, skip to the next step.
  6. Add the turmeric powder, ground cumin, ground coriander, black pepper, and paprika to the pot. Mix well to coat the meat or onions with the spices.
  7. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and release their juices.
  8. Add the soaked and drained rice to the pot. Stir well to coat the rice with the onion and spice mixture.
  9. Pour in the water or chicken/beef broth and season with salt according to your taste. Bring it to a boil.
  10. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed. If using meat, ensure it is cooked through and tender.
  11. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5 minutes. This will allow the steam to finish cooking the rice and make it fluffy.
  12. Fluff the rice gently with a fork.
  13. Serve the Kenyan-style pilau hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves if desired. Squeeze some lemon juice over the pilau for added freshness and tanginess.

This pilau recipe can serve approximately 4 to 6 people, depending on the portion size and individual appetites. Adjustments can be made to the quantities of ingredients based on the number of servings desired.
